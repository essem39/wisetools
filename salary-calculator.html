Salary Calculations in Europe — Guide

Gross vs Net Salary — What's the Difference?

The gross salary is the total amount agreed in an employment contract before any deductions. The net salary is what the employee actually receives after income tax and social contributions are deducted. In most EU countries, the difference between gross and net can be substantial — in Belgium for example, an employee earning €3,000 gross takes home approximately €1,900 net.

Salary Calculator Belgium — Highest Tax in EU

Belgium has one of the highest tax burdens in the EU. An employee pays 13.07% in personal social security contributions, plus income tax at progressive rates from 25% to 50%. On a €3,000 gross monthly salary, the effective total deduction is approximately 37%. Employer costs add another 27% on top of gross, making Belgium expensive for hiring.

Salary Calculator Germany — Steuer & Sozialabgaben

Germany applies income tax (Einkommensteuer) at rates from 14% to 45%, plus a solidarity surcharge (Solidaritätszuschlag) and optional church tax. Social contributions (Sozialabgaben) cover health insurance (~7.3%), pension (~9.3%), unemployment (~1.3%), and long-term care (~1.525%). Total employee deductions typically range from 35–42% of gross salary.

Salary Calculator France — Cotisations Sociales

France has complex social contribution system. Employee contributions (cotisations salariales) typically amount to 20–25% of gross, covering health, retirement, unemployment, and supplementary pension. Income tax (impôt sur le revenu) is calculated at the household level annually. Our calculator uses simplified effective rates as an approximation.

Remote Work and Cross-Border Payroll

With remote work, many EU businesses hire employees in lower-tax countries like Poland, Romania, or Bulgaria while operating from Belgium or Germany. This requires understanding both the employee's local tax obligations and the employer's payroll registration requirements in each country. Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this salary calculator?
This calculator provides approximate results based on simplified effective tax rates. It is suitable for quick estimations and comparisons between countries. For precise payroll calculations — especially for actual salary processing — consult a certified accountant or payroll specialist familiar with local tax law.
What is the minimum wage in Belgium in 2026?
The national minimum wage in Belgium (salaire minimum / minimumloon) is approximately €2,070 gross per month in 2026, making it one of the highest in the EU. This is the RMMMG (Revenu Minimum Mensuel Moyen Garanti) set through collective bargaining.
Does the 13th month salary affect Belgian tax calculations?
Yes. In Belgium, the 13th month bonus (and holiday pay — pécule de vacances) is subject to tax and social contributions. Holiday pay is often taxed separately at a special rate. Our calculator shows monthly estimates based on base salary only and does not account for year-end bonuses.
How do I pay salaries to employees in different EU countries?
You need to register as an employer in each country where employees are resident, or use an Employer of Record (EOR) service. For the actual salary payment transfers, Wise Business allows you to send EUR and other currencies to local bank accounts at minimal cost — useful for remote teams across the EU.
