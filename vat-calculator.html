How to Add VAT to a Price

To add VAT to a net price, multiply by (1 + VAT rate). For example, €100 in Belgium with 21% VAT: €100 × 1.21 = €121 (incl. VAT). The VAT amount is €21.

How to Remove VAT from a Price

To remove VAT from a gross price, divide by (1 + VAT rate). For example, €121 ÷ 1.21 = €100 (excl. VAT). The VAT portion is €21. Use "Remove VAT" mode above.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the VAT rate in Belgium in 2026?
Belgium's standard VAT rate remains 21% in 2026. Reduced rates are 12% (restaurants, phyto-pharmaceutical products) and 6% (food, water, books, newspapers, medicines, certain construction). The zero rate applies to some periodic publications and intra-community supplies.
How do I calculate VAT-exclusive price from a VAT-inclusive price?
Divide the VAT-inclusive price by (1 + VAT rate). Example with German 19% VAT: €119 ÷ 1.19 = €100 (net price). The VAT portion is €19. Use the "Remove VAT" mode in our calculator for instant results.
Which EU country has the highest VAT rate?
Hungary has the EU's highest standard VAT rate at 27%. Sweden, Denmark, and Finland all apply 25% (Finland 25.5%). Luxembourg has the lowest standard rate at 17%.
