❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wise Business really free to open?
Yes — there is no fee to open a Wise Business account. The basic account has no monthly maintenance fee. You pay only for transactions: a small fee per transfer (typically 0.3–0.7% of the transfer amount, much lower than banks). Some premium features like batch payments have additional costs. See current pricing at wise.com/pricing.
Is Wise regulated in Belgium and the EU?
Yes. Wise is authorized by the National Bank of Belgium (NBB) as an Electronic Money Institution. This means Wise is a regulated financial institution under EU law. Your funds are safeguarded — Wise holds customer money in separate accounts with top-tier banks, protected even if Wise were to face financial difficulties.
What is the difference between Wise Personal and Wise Business?
Wise Business is specifically designed for companies, freelancers, and entrepreneurs. It offers higher transfer limits, team member access with permissions, dedicated business bank details in multiple countries, accounting software integrations (Xero, QuickBooks), batch payment capabilities, and business-specific customer support. Personal accounts are for individual use only.
Can I use Wise Business to pay VAT and taxes?
Yes. You can transfer EUR from your Wise Business account to any EU bank account, including tax authority accounts. Many EU entrepreneurs use Wise to pay their quarterly OSS VAT declarations, annual corporate tax, and social security contributions. The process is identical to a regular bank transfer — just use the tax authority's IBAN and reference number.
Does Wise Business provide a Belgian IBAN for receiving payments?
Yes. Wise Business provides a genuine Belgian IBAN (starting with BE) that works for all SEPA credit transfers and direct debits. Your clients can pay you as if you had a regular Belgian bank account. You also get bank details for the UK, US, Australia, Canada, and several other countries to receive payments like a local.
