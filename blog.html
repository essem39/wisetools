Understanding VAT in the EU

If you sell goods or services in the European Union, VAT (Value Added Tax) is unavoidable. Rates vary from 17% in Luxembourg to 27% in Hungary. Knowing where and when to register is critical to avoid penalties.

Thresholds and Registration

Each EU country has its own threshold for VAT registration. For cross-border sales, the €10,000 EU-wide threshold applies. Once exceeded, you must register for OSS (One-Stop Shop) or IOSS (Import One-Stop Shop).

Reverse Charge Mechanism

For B2B services across borders, the reverse charge shifts VAT liability to the buyer. This means you invoice without VAT and the buyer accounts for it locally. Incorrect wording on invoices can lead to rejected VAT deductions.

OSS – One-Stop Shop

For EU-based sellers of goods and digital services. You report and pay VAT for all EU sales in one country.

IOSS – Import One-Stop Shop

For non-EU sellers shipping goods worth ≤€150 to EU consumers. VAT is collected at checkout and remitted monthly.
