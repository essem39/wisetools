EU Currency Converter — Complete Guide

Our EU currency converter uses reference rates published by the European Central Bank (ECB) — the official source for euro exchange rates. These rates are published daily at approximately 16:00 CET and are used as reference rates for accounting, contracts, and tax purposes across the European Union.

Whether you're converting EUR to USD for an international invoice, checking PLN to EUR for a Polish supplier, or calculating GBP amounts for UK clients post-Brexit, this tool covers the most important currency pairs for European business.

EUR to USD — Most Traded Pair in Europe

The EUR/USD pair is the world's most traded currency pair. For EU businesses selling to US clients or paying US suppliers, this conversion is a daily operation. The ECB reference rate is the standard used in EU accounting and VAT calculations for foreign currency invoices.

EUR to GBP — Post-Brexit Considerations

Since Brexit, UK-EU trade requires careful currency management. Invoices between EU and UK companies must clearly state the currency and often include the exchange rate used. Our converter reflects current ECB reference rates for GBP, making it suitable for cross-border invoice preparation.

EUR to PLN, CZK, HUF — Central European Currencies

Poland, Czech Republic, and Hungary remain outside the eurozone despite EU membership. This makes EUR/PLN, EUR/CZK, and EUR/HUF conversions essential for businesses operating across Central Europe. Our tool supports all three with live indicative rates.

Why ECB Rates Matter for EU Business

  • VAT reporting requires converting foreign currency invoices at ECB rates
  • EU accounting standards (IFRS, local GAAP) reference ECB rates
  • Customs declarations use ECB rates for import value calculations
  • Contract disputes often resolved using ECB reference rates

Frequently Asked Questions

What exchange rates does this converter use?
This tool uses indicative rates based on ECB (European Central Bank) reference rates as a baseline. For live trading rates and actual transfers, we recommend using Wise, which provides the real mid-market rate with full transparency.
What is the ECB reference rate?
The ECB reference rate is the official euro exchange rate published by the European Central Bank each business day around 16:00 CET. It is computed from a daily concertation procedure between central banks. It is used as the standard rate for EU accounting, VAT reporting, and customs valuation.
How do I convert foreign currency invoices for VAT purposes?
For EU VAT returns, foreign currency amounts must be converted to EUR using the ECB reference rate on the date of the taxable transaction (or the date of the invoice, depending on country rules). Keep a record of the rate used for each conversion as tax authorities may audit this.
